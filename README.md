# Barriers to Housing Supply: Four Case Studies from Hong Kong

An interactive longform article examining the persistent barriers to housing construction in Hong Kong — one of the world's most expensive housing markets.

## About the Project

By 2018, the shortfall of public and private flats in Hong Kong had accumulated to over 70,000 units due to a chronic lack of available development sites (Housing Authority). This project investigates the factors slowing housing construction through in-depth case studies, with a primary focus on the redevelopment of the former Kai Tak Airport.

The article is presented as an immersive, scroll-driven multimedia story built with [Shorthand](https://shorthand.com/), featuring original photomontages, interactive maps, data visualisations, and on-the-ground photography.

## Case Study: Kai Tak Development

The centrepiece case study examines why the former Kai Tak Airport — closed in 1998 — remained largely a vast construction site over two decades later. The investigation covers:

- **Prolonged Zoning Process** — Multiple revisions to the Outline Zoning Plan from 1998 to 2018, with the Kai Tak OZP not approved until 2007
- **Inconsistent Population Projections** — The planned population swung from 320,000 (1998) to 105,000 (2007) to 134,000 (2017)
- **Residential Density Debates** — Long-standing disagreements between high- and low-density advocates, especially for the former runway area
- **Archaeological Discoveries** — Lung Tsun Stone Bridge remnants unearthed in 2008 further stalled planning approval
- **Slow Release of Sites for Tender** — 80% of residential parcels remained unsold as of May 2019
- **Transport Infrastructure Gaps** — Delays to the Shatin to Central Link and insufficient bus services

The article draws on interviews with District Councillors, environmental advocates, and government responses, alongside data from the Housing Authority, Lands Department, Town Planning Board, and Legislative Council.

## Visual & Interactive Features

- **Author-drawn photomontages** illustrating the proposed redevelopment based on the Kai Tak Planning Review
- **Scrollmation and reveal sections** for before/after comparisons
- **Embedded data visualisations** (Infogram) showing housing supply statistics, land sale timelines, and population projections
- **Interactive photo galleries** with captioned images from WikiCommons, Flickr, and original photography
- **Annotated Outline Zoning Plan maps** (1998–2018) tracing the evolution of planning decisions
